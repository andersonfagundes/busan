Discover 3 must-visit destinations in Busan

Busan's attractions range from centuries-old Buddhist temples scattered across the city's mountains and coastline to pristine beaches with crystal-clear waters. This coastal city offers plenty to do year-round—families can spend time at a seaside aquarium, shoppers can explore vibrant neighborhoods, and nature lovers can enjoy long hikes to scenic viewpoints. The Buddhist temples dotting Busan's coast and mountains feature impressive architecture that will delight photographers.

1.  Haedong Yonggungsa Temple

The Haedong Yonggungsa Temple is a Buddhist temple located in the northeastern part of Busan. Built in 1376, it is one of the few temples in Korea constructed by the seaside – you can enjoy views of the East Sea on one side and beautiful mountains on the other.

Good for

  • History

2.  Beomeosa Temple

Beomeosa Temple is one of the largest sanctuaries in South Korea. It is located high on the eastern edge of Geumjeong Mountain and is far from the hustle and bustle of the city. The Daeungjeon Hall of the temple is a well-preserved example of Joseon Dynasty architecture.

Good for

  • History

3.  Yongdusan Park

Yongdusan Park, located in the center of Busan, houses some of the city’s most important monuments. You can see spectacular views from the top of the 120-meter-high Busan Tower. The park has 2 museums – check out the traditional musical instruments at the Museum of World Folk Instruments and over 80 Korean sailboats at the Exhibition Hall of World Model Boats.

Good for

  • Couples
  • Families
  • Budget
